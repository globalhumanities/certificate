Certificate

The Global Humanities Certificate invites students to step beyond borders—intellectual, cultural, and creative—to explore what it means to be human in a rapidly changing world. Through interdisciplinary study of literature, art, philosophy, and history, the program encourages critical thinking and creativity by engaging students with global perspectives in three main areas: how cultures shape ideas, how imagination and the arts connect people, and how stories and histories reveal and reshape our collective identities.

Mission statement

Global Humanities certificate offers students the opportunity to step outside their own traditions and see the world from distinct global perspectives. Exploring ideas and artifacts – literary, visual, material, artistic, etc. – in varied global contexts, the certificate will open the humanities to all students across the network, including those in other fields. Incorporating the array of disciplines and approaches that comprise the humanities, its courses explore the human experience through different global lenses and help students engage with and navigate an increasingly complex global context. Familiarity with the humanities gives students a richer and more nuanced basis for their future work and contribution as citizens, offering an ethical, aesthetic, and historical foundation for understanding contemporary global problems and ways of living.
